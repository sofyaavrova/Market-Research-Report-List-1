<p><p>Nonionic low-foaming surfactants market types include ethoxylates, fatty acid esters of polyhydroxy compounds, amine oxides, and others. Ethoxylates are created through the process of ethoxylation, which involves adding ethylene oxide to a fatty alcohol or acid. Fatty acid esters of polyhydroxy compounds are derived from fatty acids and sugar alcohols. Amine oxides are produced through oxidation of tertiary amines. The "others" category encompasses various nonionic surfactants, such as silicone-based surfactants. These types of nonionic low-foaming surfactants are widely used in formulations where foam generation must be minimized or eliminated, such as in cleaning products or personal care items.</p></p>
