<p><p>The Food Grade Silica Market consists of two main types: Precipitated Silica and Fumed Silica. Precipitated Silica is a finely divided form of silica that is produced by a chemical process. It is widely used as an additive in food products to enhance anti-caking properties and improve texture. On the other hand, Fumed Silica is a highly pure form of amorphous silica, produced by high-temperature flame hydrolysis. It is commonly used as a thickening agent, anti-settling agent, and reinforcing filler in various food applications. Both types play vital roles in ensuring quality and functionality in food products.</p></p>
